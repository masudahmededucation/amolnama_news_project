No. They do different things:

Background remover — needs to detect the subject (person/object) and separate it from the background. Uses segmentation AI (like rembg/U2Net). LaMa can't do this — it doesn't know what's "foreground" vs "background."

LaMa — fills in holes/masked areas with reconstructed texture. It's for removing unwanted objects (watermarks, blemishes, power lines) from an image while keeping everything else.

Your background remover already uses the right approach (likely rembg or similar segmentation model). LaMa would make it worse, not better.
