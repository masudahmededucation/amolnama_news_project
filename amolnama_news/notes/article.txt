I want to have a two-column layout for my news article. 
On the left column, I want to highlight key words, date, place, and key figures. 
On the right column, I want the main news content including the headline. 
For every paragraph on the right, if there are any keywords, date, 
or place found in that paragraph, they should appear on the left. 
How can I approach this in the UI, database, and article view?


This is a high-end editorial design often used by premium news outlets like 
The New York Times or The Economist. It’s called a "Sidenote" or "Margin Data" architecture.

To keep it "light and fast" for your Django/MS SQL app, 
you need a way to link specific metadata to specific paragraphs.


To structure your news article with a two-column layout, 
store the article content in your database as a single block of text, 
then split it into paragraphs when displaying it. On the right column, 
show the full article with its headline and paragraphs. On the left column, 
analyze each corresponding paragraph to automatically extract and highlight any dates, 
places, or key names—you can do this in the user interface using JavaScript,
 or beforehand in the database by saving metadata like keywords per paragraph. 
 When rendering the article view, loop through the paragraphs, display each one on the right, 
 and on the left show only the extracted keywords, date, and person for that same paragraph.
