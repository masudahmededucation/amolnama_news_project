Why this works (The "Secret Sauce" for 2026):
If you want to know why those specific terms matter, here is the breakdown:

SSR/SSG: Standard "apps" are often invisible to Google because they are just a blank script. This forces the server to send real text that Google can read instantly.

Atomic Answers: AI search engines (like the one you are using now) love 50-word summaries at the top of sections. It makes it easy for the AI to "quote" your app as a source.

llms.txt: This is a new 2026 standard. It’s like a robots.txt but specifically for AI. It tells models like GPT-5 or Gemini exactly what your app is about so they recommend you to users.

INP (Interaction to Next Paint): This measures that "mouse stuck" feeling you mentioned earlier. Keeping this low ensures Google sees your app as high-quality.
